E-Create

University Project

Overview

On e-Create, users can search for recipes on how to turn old, unwanted items into something new. One example can be a recipe to turn an old clock into a new picture frame.

A University project of mine, I kept this on my portfolio because this was the project where I learned the importance of Usability Testing and Paper Prototyping.

You can view the high-fidelity prototype here.

Learning the Process

This project taught me the importance of having a formal process for my designs. My team and I would do user research, create user tasks and scenarios, storyboards, and also created a low and hi-fidelity prototype.

Here you can see a storyboard from one of our user scenarios and here is a link to our Design Proposal, which includes details on the formal processes mentioned above.

Paper Prototyping and Testing

This project also gave me experience with Paper Prototyping and Usability Testing at every step of the way.

Here you can see a video of a student trying out our prototype which gave us extremely valuable and obvious insight into how our site will be used.

You can also view the full testing report for all of our users, task scenarios, and results.

Hi-Fi Prototype

For the last part of our project, we created and tested a high-fidelity prototype. This website had a few working examples on it, but due to time constraints, we excluded the functionality of posting a recipe, and focused more on how users will find a recipe.

You can view the high-fidelity prototype here.

Outcome

On top of all of the knowledge gained from this project, we got an A+ in this course!
