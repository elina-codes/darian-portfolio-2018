shirt wrap

Created by Jamie Lee

Create a wrap from a button-down shirt. The trick to this easy DIY project is choosing the right shirt. For a grungy look, go with something casual. For an upscale wrap, choose a blouse with a classy design, or made of fine materials like satin.


Supplies:
A button-down shirt, either long or short sleeved
Scissors
Measuring tape
Chalk


Procedure:
1. Put on the shirt and mark two inches below your bust with the chalk. Take off the shirt and repeat this mark across the shirt so that you have a nice line to cut.
2. Lay your shirt out on the floor. Cut the shirt across your chalk line. Make sure you are cutting through the front and back of the shirt.
This is great for a grunge look, but if you want a more polished look, you can hem the edge.
3. You don't have to hem your wrap, but if you are going for a more polished look, this will make the wrap look finished.
4. Turn up the bottom of the wrap about a 1/2" and pin it in place. Iron the hem to make a nice crease. Sew it a 1/8" from the edge.
5. T his wrap looks fantastic with a simple flowing skirt that you can make yourself.
